Organismal Thermal Stress

by Tony Cannistra

Certain animals, like snakes and lizards, rely on the temperature of their environment to provide energy for life.

Because they can't regulate their own temperature, they are faced with "the goldilocks problem:" their environment has to be just right.

When the organism's body temperature exceeds that "goldilocks" range, they are considered to be in "thermal stress." This visualization uses modeled body temperatures to visualize areas of thermal stress in Colorado.
